Terms:

  • Poetic language: Is a language which says more with less. Compact, concise, impactful

  • Denotation: Literal use of the language, for example the dictionary meanings

  • Connotation: Figurative use of the language, for example a metaphor

  • Accent: Where a word stress is

  • Meter: If a language is metrical there is a pattern of accented syllable, like a consistent rhythm

  • Foot: In Turkish Uyak. Means the basic metrical unit

  • Iamb: A foot of two syllables where the second syllable is accented. For example the word 'to-day'

    1. Iambic Monometer
    2. Iambic Dimeter
    3. Iambic Trimeter
    4. Iambic Tetrameter
    5. Iambic Pentameter / Commonly used

  • Stanza: Basic units of division

  • Lines: Sentences in poem

  • Rhyme: The repetition of the accented vowel sound and all the other sounds that follow it

  • Internal Rhyme: A rhyme that is at the start or the middle of the line

  • End Rhyme: A rhyme that is at the end of the line

  • Free Verse: If there is no metrical pattern and no rhyme

  • Blank Verse: If there is no rhyme but there is a pattern, and that pattern is Iambic

  • Subject: What a text is about, usually short and general

  • Theme: What the text tells us about the subject. Goes more into the subject

  • End-stopped line: A metrical line containing a complete phrase or sentence, or a poetic line ending with punctuation

  • Run-on line (enjambment): When the end of the line is not marked by punctuation, and the sentence or clause carries on over into the next line

  • Personification: Talking about a non-human as a person

  • The poetic persona/speaker: The word writer isn't used in poems, instead poetic persona or speaker is used

  • Simile: Likeining something to something else. For example, like a thunderbolt he falls

  • Sonnet: A fourteen-line poem

    • Quatrain: A stanza of four lines

    • Couplet: Two lines of poetry at the end of a sonnet, that have the same length and rhyme pattern (RESEARCH)

  • Metonymy: It's a rhetorical device where something is referred to by the name of something closely related or associated with it

    • For example, "When you say we swear by the crown." you mean that you swear to the royal family.

  • Synecdoche: When a part of something is used to refer to the whole

    • For example, when you say 'Do you like my new wheels?' you are asking that if you like my new car.

  • The difference between metaphor & simile:

    • You are a rose is a metaphor and you are like a rose is a simile

  • Symbol: Is an evocative kind of image which has some further significance associated with it.

    • How does it differ from a metaphor: A symbol is open to multiple interpretations while a metophor is quite clear. Another difference is that a metaphor functions figuratively while a symbol functions both figuratively and literally.

  • Alliteration: Repetition of initial consonant sounds in neighboring words or syllables

    • "Safe and sound", "Tried and true", "Claps the crag with crooked hands"

  • Consonance: Repetition of the final consonant sounds in neighboring words or syllables

    • "First and last", "Short and sweet"

  • Assonance: Repetition of the vowel sounds in neighboring words or syllables

    • "Free and easy", "Rise high in the bright sky"
